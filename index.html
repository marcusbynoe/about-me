About Me

Biography

Hey there my name is Marcus Bynoe! I was born in Virginia, but since my father was in the military I grew up around the country. Though, I did spend a great portion of my life growing up in Colorado Springs, CO and Oahu, HI. So I consider Colorado and Hawai'i my "home" states. My education history is a little bit all over the place. I finished my high school years in Hawai'i and started college at Leeward Community College. After a year I decided to drop out as I did not know what I wanted to do. After a 4 year stint in the U.S Army I moved to Colorado and enrolled at Metro State University of Denver. After two semesters there, I transferred to Community College of Aurora as the university style was a bit overwhelming for me. At CCA, I was studying for my degree in cybersecurity until the pandemic happened in 2020. Since then, I've put my education on hold up until enrolling in Code Fellows.

My job experience is mainly centered around logistics. In the Army, I was a transportation management coordinator for 4 years. After that I was a transportation coordinator for Iron Mountain; a shredding company. After that I was a logistics specialist for an aerospace company called Sierra Nevada Corporation. After spending a year there, I moved back to Hawai'i to spend some time on the islands again. From there I was a janitor at the Hawai'i State Hospital (which was a psychiatric hospital for court ordered individuals) and then a TSA officer on the island of Maui.

My main goal right now is to make a career transition into tech. I've always loved computers and technology, especially after I built my first gaming desktop in 2011 as a teenager. I feel that being a software developer will provide many challenging and exciting opportunities. Which in turn, will help me achieve a better quality of life in the end.
